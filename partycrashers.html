PARTY CRASHERS Characters

Community maintained character wiki page. Information may be incomplete due to resets.

Lux

Character Info
Name Lux
HP 100
Role Survivor

Overview: Lux is a small child trapped inside PARTY CRASHERS after entering through a dream. They rely on hope and survival instincts rather than strength.

Lore: Lux believes the world is not real and holds onto the idea that escape is possible someday. Each reset leaves emotional traces even when memories fade.

  • Dash: Panic driven movement forward.
  • Cover: Defensive fear that punishes attackers.

Toko

Character Info
Name Toko
HP 150
Role Fighter

Overview: Toko faces PARTY CRASHERS with confidence and aggression, choosing control over fear.

Lore: Instead of hoping for escape, Toko focuses on mastering the rules of the world and surviving on their own terms.

  • Slash: High risk attack that rewards commitment.
  • Parry: Defensive stance that turns attacks back on enemies.

Medic

Character Info
Name Medic
HP 100
Role Support

Overview: Medic keeps others alive despite constant fear, relying on speed and awareness rather than combat.

Lore: Fear drives Medic forward, turning panic into survival. They believe teamwork is the only path to escape.

Evil Lux

Character Info
Name Evil Lux
HP 666
Role Killer
Damage 25 (M1)

Evil Lux as they appear in-game.

Overview

Evil Lux is a twisted version of Lux that appears as a killer within PARTY CRASHERS. Unlike the original, this version is aggressive, relentless, and seems aware of the dream in a disturbing way.

Lore

Some players believe Evil Lux is what happens when hope fails after too many resets. The familiar appearance hides something far more dangerous, suggesting the dream can change survivors into hunters.

Evil Lux moves with purpose rather than fear, hunting players by teleporting directly toward them, as if drawn by their presence. Their ability to regenerate hints that they are deeply connected to the rules of the world itself.

No one knows if Evil Lux is truly Lux corrupted, a separate entity wearing their shape, or a manifestation of the dream reacting to players who try to escape.

Abilities (Lore Explanation)

  • Teleport: Instantly closes distance, reinforcing the feeling that escape is never guaranteed.
  • Regen: Standing still allows Evil Lux to recover, creating tense moments where players must decide whether to interrupt or run.
  • Beatdown: A violent forward charge that punishes players caught in its path.

Personality

Evil Lux appears cold, intense, and driven, showing none of the hesitation or fear seen in the survivor version.

Trivia

  • The HP value of 666 has led many fans to assume Evil Lux represents a corrupted or nightmare version of Lux.
  • Some players claim Evil Lux targets Lux first when both appear in the same match.
  • Fans often debate whether Evil Lux remembers past timelines.

Homer (Developer Joke Character)

Character Info
Name Homer
HP Unknown
Role Survivor
Availability DEVELOPERS ONLY

Developer joke character used for testing and debugging.

IMPORTANT NOTE

This character is NOT a normal playable character. Homer exists only for developers and testing purposes. Players cannot unlock or select this character in normal gameplay.

Overview

Homer is a joke survivor inspired by Homer Simpson and designed as an intentionally overpowered testing character. He is used by developers to stress test mechanics, balance changes, and extreme gameplay situations.

Lore (Non-Canon)

Within the community, Homer is treated as a glitch or anomaly inside PARTY CRASHERS. Some players describe him as a character that “does not follow the rules,” appearing only when developers interfere with the game world.

Because he is not part of the main story, any appearances are considered non-canon and purely for debugging or humor.

Abilities (Developer Use)

  • Donut Blast: Designed to survive nearly any encounter for testing.
  • Self Heal: Allows developers to quickly heal themselves. Heals around 2890 HP.
  • Debug Mobility: Often used to move through dangerous areas safely.

Personality

Presented as humorous and out of place compared to other characters, reinforcing that he is not part of the serious narrative.

Trivia

  • Considered a “dev-only” character and should not appear in normal matches.
  • Sometimes referenced in patch notes as an internal testing tool.
  • Community rumors about unlocking Homer are false.

Style Notes

  • Lux & Medic: Players often pair Lux with Medic because Lux’s defensive abilities create safe moments for healing. Many fans see them as representing hope and protection.
  • Lux & Toko: Lux avoids direct fights while Toko runs toward danger, making them feel like opposites that balance each other during matches.
  • Toko & Medic: Toko’s aggressive playstyle gives Medic opportunities to support from behind, although some players joke that Medic spends most runs chasing Toko around the map.
  • Evil Lux & Lux: A major fan theory claims Evil Lux is a corrupted future version of Lux created by too many resets, though others believe it is simply the dream copying survivors.
  • Evil Lux Behavior: Players report that Evil Lux feels more aware than other killers, especially due to teleporting directly toward targets.
  • Team Symbolism Theory: Many fans interpret the survivors as emotional archetypes: hope (Lux), defiance (Toko), and fear-driven care (Medic).
  • Reset Theory: Some players claim subtle animation or behavior changes suggest characters evolve across multiple resets.
  • Unknown Connection: A popular speculation suggests Lux, Toko, and Medic may have known each other before entering PARTY CRASHERS, but no confirmed lore exists.
  • Homer (Developer Character): Homer is confirmed to be a developer-only joke character and is NOT obtainable in normal gameplay. Any sightings in public matches are assumed to be developer testing or bugs.
  • Homer Rumors: Community rumors about secret unlock methods or hidden codes to play as Homer are false according to developer notes.

Community Speculation

  • Some fans believe Evil Lux exists to punish players who rely too heavily on Lux strategies.
  • There is debate over whether the dream world creates killers from survivors or if killers exist independently.
  • A recurring theory suggests hidden events may occur when Lux, Toko, Medic, and Evil Lux appear within the same match.
  • Developers occasionally referencing Homer in updates has led to jokes that he is "watching from outside the game."

These notes may contain outdated information due to resets, patches, or developer testing.
